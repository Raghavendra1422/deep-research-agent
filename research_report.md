The Indian semiconductor market is dominated by key players such as Intel, Samsung Electronics, Qualcomm, Micron Technology, and Texas Instruments. The market is expected to witness significant competition, with new players entering the market and existing players expanding their operations.

## 3. Demand Drivers & Applications
The demand for AI semiconductors in India is driven by various factors, including the growing demand for AI and advanced technologies. The market is driven by applications such as 5G/6G, data centers, and AI, with 80% of Indian firms viewing AI as a key growth driver. The market is expected to be driven by the growing demand for AI-powered devices, including smartphones, laptops, and servers. The Indian government's initiatives, such as the Scheme for setting up of Semiconductor Fabs in India, are expected to provide fiscal support of up to 50% of project cost, driving the growth of the market.

## 4. Government Policies & Initiatives
The Indian government has announced various initiatives to strengthen the semiconductor industry, including ISM 2.0, the next phase of the initiative to strengthen the semiconductor industry. The government has committed funding for the semiconductor industry, with 97% of the funding already committed by August 2025. The Indian government has also announced a joint initiative on Critical and Emerging Technology (iCET) with the United States, expected to drive the growth of the market.
